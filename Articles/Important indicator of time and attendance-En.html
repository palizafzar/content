The capacity of log storage in a time and attendance system:

  • Time and attendance devices have various types and prices. It depends on the storage capacity and the number of personnel's logs. If your staff's number is more than 100 people, keep in mind that you should choose a device that has a high capacity to store records.
  • Pay attention to the below example:
  • Suppose 100 employees are working at your company, and on average, every person registers their log four times a day; therefore, you will have 400 records a day. If you want to provide a monthly report from your personnel--including 31 days-- you must select a device with a capacity of at least 12,400.

Available communication ports in a time and attendance machine:

  • To communicate a time attendance device with a system that a time & attendance software--web or Windows--is installed on it, there are several ports to receiving data for monthly reporting of personnel and transfer them to a payroll system. The most popular ports are LAN, Wi-Fi, USB, SD Card, RS232, and RS485.
  • LAN: Communication through the LAN is appropriate for transmitting time attendance data in small buildings or companies.
  • Wi-Fi: Using the Wi-Fi on the time attendance devices, administrators can communicate online with servers and transfer device information.
  • USB: If you don't have a network connection, you can transfer offline information from devices through a flash memory.
  • SD Card: Using the SD Card is the same as using USB to transfer offline information.
