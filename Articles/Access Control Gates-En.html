Access control gates are an essential component of modern security systems. They provide a reliable way to monitor and restrict access to restricted areas, buildings, and properties. In this guide, we'll examine the different types of access control gates, their benefits, and what to consider when choosing access control systems.

Types of Access Control Gates

Biometric Gates

These gates incorporate biometric technology, such as fingerprint or facial recognition, for access control. They are commonly used in high-security areas where precise identification is required.

Turnstiles Gates

Turnstiles are commonly used in stadiums, public transportation systems, and office buildings. They allow only one person to pass through at a time and often require a ticket or access card for entry.

Turnstile Gates

Speed Gates

Speed gates are commonly used in airports, train stations, and office buildings with high pedestrian traffic. They are designed to allow quick passage while preventing tailgating or unauthorized entry.

Speed Gates

Optical Turnstiles

Optical turnstiles use infrared sensors to detect when someone enters or exits. They are commonly used in office buildings, embassies, and other locations requiring a higher level of security.

Swing Gates

Swing gates operate similarly to regular doors but are motorized and usually require a keycard or code for entry. They are commonly used in residential areas and commercial buildings.

Sliding Gates

Sliding gates slide open horizontally to allow vehicles or pedestrians access. They are commonly used in large entrances such as commercial properties or high-security areas.

Boom Barriers / Gates

Boom barriers are commonly used in parking lots and toll booths. They consist of a long arm that can be raised or lowered to allow or restrict access.

Revolving Gates / Door

Revolving door consist of rotating barriers that allow only one person to enter or exit at a time. They are commonly used in high-security areas or areas with high traffic flow.

Benefits of Using Access Control Gates

  • Improved Security: Access control gates help to restrict unauthorized entry into restricted areas, improving overall security.
  • Convenience: Access control systems are convenient for those who need to access restricted areas frequently.
  • Increased Property Value: Having an access control system installed can increase the value of a property, creating an added level of security and privacy.
  • Customizable: Access control systems can be customized to meet the specific needs of the property, including different gate types, access levels, and integration with other security systems.

Installation and Maintenance of Access Control Gates

Installation of access control gates requires expertise and experience. It is important to choose a certified dealer for installation and maintenance of the access control system. Routine maintenance is vital to ensure the system remains in top working condition and to address any potential issues that may arise.
