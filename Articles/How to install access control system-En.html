Checking access control Virdi AC2100 Plus wires

An access control Virdi AC2100 Plus has formed a series of colored wires on the back panel. This bunch of cables is divided into six general groups.

  • The first group is the network cable, which consists of four wires called # 6 [c], # 2 [0], # [w / 0], and # 3 [w / 6]. This cable is used to TCP/IP network.
  • The second group consists of two wires called GND / Ground and +12v connected to a socket. This socket will be connected to an adapter to power the device.
  • The Third group consists of EXT (Exit), DM0, DM1, and GND wires. If the traffic on the other side of the door does not look important to who opens the door, we will use the exit key by connecting the EXT and GND wires from a device to an exit key.
  • The fourth group consists of seven wires called GND, W11, W10, W01, W00, R4B, and R4A. Suppose we want to control an electronic door from both sides. In this case, we need two devices for controlling the traffic on both sides of the door. The primary and secondary access control device control one entity and connect to a single database. Accordingly, we need to establish a relationship between the two devices. We use R4B and R4A wires the same as RS485 communication due to communicating with each other.
  • The fifth group includes two wires called GND and +12v, which are without a socket.
  • The sixth group consists of seven wires called L1C, L1NC (Normal Close), L1NO (Normal Open), L2, and two GNDs.

Introducing magnetic locks

Palizafzar’s magnetic locks consist of two output wires in black and red. Red wire requires 12 volts, and the black wire is used for connecting GND (Ground).

To connect a fingerprint access control system and a magnetic lock, it’s most desirable to use separate adapters. As a result, If the adapter of the fingerprint access control system may have been damaged, the electronic door will keep in work continuously. The electronic door will continue to operate continuously without interruption because the Magnet Lock uses a separate adapter.

Why do we need to use separate adapters?

  • Suppose that you use just an adapter for magnetic lock and access control Virdi AC2100 Plus, use an adapter, and the corresponding wiring on the back of the traffic control device. In this case, there is a risk that an electric-magnetic lock always sends 12V output to the access control system. For this reason, we use separate adapters.
  • Note: Since the electronic door always has a 12 voltage on it and fingering, the relay disconnects communication for a few seconds until the door is opened. Keep in mind that with disconnecting the leading electricity of the building, doors always would remain as open. Therefore, it is recommended that you use UPS to maintain security.
