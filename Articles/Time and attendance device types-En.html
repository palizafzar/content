  • Trends on Time and Attendance Devices

Time & attendance devices are used to track entrance and exit personnel at work. ADP time and attendance systems enable employers to monitor their employees' work hours and late arrivals, early departures, time taken on breaks, and absenteeism. Time and attendance systems are divided into two general categories: manual and automatic. Manual systems were based on papers that personnel's entrance and exit time were printed on it. Using these systems was a very laborious task in calculating salaries, and sometimes it took a few days to figure the compensation. In addition, delays in the payment of staff salaries caused them dissatisfaction. With the advent of computers, card, fingerprint, face, and iris access control systems were introduced, respectively.

Punch Time Clock

The first time and attendance devices were punching time & attendance devices. The date and time of personnel entrance or exit were registered on the card by placing the card in a particular slot. These cards were tabulated from days of the month that each day was split into two sections for entrance and exit. These cards were collected at the end of the month to calculate working hours and late arrivals, early departures, time taken on breaks, and absenteeism. Calculating those times for organizations with many employees was very trying, and sometimes it took a few days.

Card Time & Attendance Systems

With the advent of computers, punch attendance devices were replaced with card time and attendance, as soon as card time & attendance devices became widespread everywhere. The work method on the initial card attendance devices was to send a code to the attendance system under DOS by dragging the card into the machine. Smart cards of card time & attendance devices include two main categories:

Smart Card Types :

  • Contact Cards:
  • Contact cards of time & attendance systems must be contacted by the device to register entrance and exit. They consist of barcodes and magnetic cards that communicate through the slot embedded in the time & attendance device.
  • Contactless Cards:
  • Contactless smart cards are located only near the time attendance device, and the physical connection is not established. Radiofrequency RF is used in these cards. Contactless cards communicate with time & attendance devices at least 10 centimeters to up.
  • The most common time & attendance cards are RFID(125KHz) cards and Mifare(13.56MHZ) cards. Mifare cards have up to 1-kilobyte storage capacity, and this information can be read and written.

Biometric Time & Attendance

Biometric technology uses human components such as fingerprints, faces, iris, palms, or voice identification. All of these components are unique in every human. The most famous biometric technology used in time & attendance devices is face recognition and fingerprint technology.

Fingerprint Time and Attendance Terminals

In fingerprint time and attendance systems, multiple personnel fingerprints are recorded. Then, when the person wants to record entrance and exit, put his finger on the sensor. His fingerprints are compared with registered fingerprints in a database then personnel is identified.

Identifying Methods of Fingerprint Time and Attendance:

  • Identify fingerprint alone
  • Identify fingerprint and password.
  • Identify fingerprint and card.

Face Time and Attendance Solutions

Face time and attendance systems check a person's identity by comparing the face images with previously registered face images.

Identifying methods of face access control systems:

  • Alone face recognition
  • Face recognition & fingerprint
  • Face recognition & card
  • Face recognition & password
  • Face recognition & fingerprint & card
  • Face recognition & fingerprint & password
  • Face recognition & fingerprint & password
  • Face recognition & card & password
  • Face recognition & fingerprint & card & password

Iris Access Control Systems

The iris is the colored, donut-shaped part of the eye behind the cornea and encloses the pupil. A person's iris pattern is individual and persists unchanged throughout life. Additionally, masked by the cornea, the iris is well shielded from damage, making it a genuine body part for biometric authentication.

Iris recognition is a method that includes examining tiny patterns in the human iris, which are unique to each individual. It is crucial to take photos with a high-quality camera to achieve detailed iris images. Iris authentication typically requires that the user remains at a prescribed location in front of a camera, which is then fixed to the position of the user's eyes.

Iris Recognition Mechanism

  • First, the pupil's location is detected, followed by detection of the iris and the eyelids.
  • Unnecessary parts (noise), including eyelids and eyelashes, are excluded from clipping out only the iris part, divided into blocks, and converted into feature values to quantify the image.
  • Matching is then completed with feature data earlier extracted and saved in the same ways.

Iris Recognition Features

  • Highly accurate and fast, iris recognition shows off having top-class precision among different biometric authentication technologies.
  • It remains constant throughout life.
  • The iris, varied between the left and right eye, can be recognized independently by each eye or identify twins.
  • As long as the eyes are revealed, iris recognition can be used even when wearing a hat, mask, hijab, or eyeglasses.
  • Despite being in a dark or at night environment identifying is excellent because of using an infrared camera.
  • Without the requirement to touch the device, contactless authentication is achievable, making it hygienic to working use.
