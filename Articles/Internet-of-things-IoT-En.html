IoT, which stands for the Internet of Things, is a new concept in technology and communication. In 1999, Kevin Ashton used the Internet of Things, which described a world in which everything, including inanimate objects, has its own digital identity, and computers allow devices, items, and gadgets to organize and manage.

Today, although the Internet connects to all people, all things are associated with IoT. In the Internet of things, billions or trillions of objects can communicate and interact with the surrounding environment to exchange information.

What Is IoT – Internet of Things?

The Internet of Things is a network that connects physical things to the Internet and other networks via uniquely identifiable IP addresses, allowing data to be collected and exchanged via embedded sensors, electronics, and software.

In IoT technology, a unique ID and an IP address are assigned for each object, which can send data to a specific database.

While the Internet of Items begins with a network of linked things, its advantages and disadvantages are mostly tied to the network technologies, systems, and applications that are developed on this underlying layer.

Things can range from consumer-oriented gadgets such as wearables and smart home solutions (Consumer IoT) to enterprise-connected equipment (Enterprise IoT) and industrial assets such as machines, robots, or even humans in smart factories and industrial facilities (Industrial IoT).

The Internet of Things technology plays a crucial role in the entrepreneurial world. Several businesses have been launched based on the Internet of Things technology. While IoT technology is at the beginning of its way, using IoT technology is a precious opportunity for entrepreneurs and creative researchers which can help to improve business and job creation.
