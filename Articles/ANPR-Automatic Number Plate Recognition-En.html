ANPR stands for Automatic Number Plate Recognition ANPR, is an exact system capable of reading vehicle number plates without human interrupting through the use of high-speed image capture with supporting illumination, pattern classification inside the images given, vehicle license plate verification of character sequences, character recognition for the image to text translation. Consequently, it would provide a metadata collection that describes an image bearing a license plate for a car and the decoded text associated with that plate.

Components of ANPR system

  • ANPR camera for taking pictures
  • ANPR software which is responsible for recognizing number plate from recorded images.
  • Sometimes and depending on the condition use of specific hardware such as camera stand, roadblock.

ANPR cameras

The most important feature of the ANPR camera is the resolution of the image. Whatever we have high-quality photos, the ability to recognize number plate will be comfortable from the resulting images. The ANPR CCTV (Closed Circuit TelevVision) is divided into two types analog and digital. The CCTV Analog's maximum resolution that you give will be 720 TVL, equivalent to 0.5 MP. These CCTV types were used for the first application with low cost in the past, But today’s mostly used digital CCTVs to achieve better results and quality.

WDR (Wide Dynamic Range)

When part of an image is too dark, but another part is so bright you cannot see any details, dynamic range, and lighting difference. Cameras with a wide dynamic range (WDR) have special software that allows them to balance that lighting for one clear image. This makes them ideal for recording areas like store entrances where the contrast between the sunshine outside and the dim lighting inside can be challenging to record.

Cameras have problems with underexposed and overexposed images because they can only accurately record the middle area of light between very dark and bright. Cameras with WDR technology have advanced sensors that can produce a broader range of lighting, allowing them to record higher light depth.

Features of an ANPR system

A strong ANPR system requires powerful and accurate parking software. If only one character mistake to be identified from 8 characters, in this case, an error occurs in ANPR. It causes the number plate to encounter an error in comparison with server information. After ensuring software, it is important to select an ANPR industrial CCTV with high precision. The ANPR system should be designed to have no problem reading number plates, and environmental conditions such as daylight and night light, rain, snow, fog, and cloudy weather do not affect it.

Nowadays, CCTV uses HLC (High Light Compensation) technology to read the number plate at night. That infrared radiates to the car's number plate at the entrance to parking so cameras can neutralize the effects of car lights and let reading number plate done easily. The range of reading ANPR varies depending on the CCTV. The highway ANPR usually supports up to 100 meters, and the parking ANPR supports about 3 to 10 meters.
